Overview

A research profile built around clarity, rigor, and useful algorithms.

I work at the intersection of mathematical analysis and empirical machine learning, with a focus on optimization methods that can scale without losing theoretical meaning.

Research vision

Build principled criteria for evaluating optimizers beyond benchmark wins alone, balancing convergence guarantees, robustness, computational cost, and implementation simplicity.

Selected work

My recent research studies dimension-independent convergence for AdaGrad with heavy-ball momentum and last-iterate convergence properties of sharpness-aware minimization.

Focus areas

Themes that shape my research program.

Adaptive optimization

Understanding how methods like AdaGrad behave in high-dimensional settings and how momentum changes their convergence properties.

Deep learning theory

Connecting optimization dynamics to practical training behavior so that theory explains, rather than merely describes, what happens in real neural network workloads.

Second-order structure

Studying what useful curvature-related objects such as Hessians, Fisher information, and covariance matrices reveal about effective optimization.

Open, reusable tools

Translating theoretical ideas into benchmarks, evaluation frameworks, and public resources that make optimizer research easier to compare and extend.
