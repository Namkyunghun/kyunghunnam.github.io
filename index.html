Overview

A research profile centered on one question: what makes a good optimizer?

I approach this question through preconditioning: how curvature, covariance, noise geometry, and loss-landscape structure should transform gradients in modern deep learning.

Research vision

Understand optimizer quality through the design of preconditioners that reshape difficult training geometry into more stable and efficient update directions.

Focus areas

A unified research map around preconditioner design.

Preconditioned optimizers

AdamW, Shampoo, SOAP, and related methods that transform gradients using diagonal, Kronecker, or spectral structure.

Curvature and covariance

Hessian, Fisher, Gauss–Newton, gradient covariance, and empirical second-moment analyses for optimizer design.

Efficient matrix computation

Inverse roots, eigendecomposition frequency, stale reuse, damping, and low-cost numerical routines for scalable preconditioning.

Loss-landscape diagnostics

Ill-conditioning, anisotropy, eigenspace drift, and training barriers that motivate targeted preconditioner design.
