Research Statement

Core Research Question: "What makes a good optimizer?"
Training neural networks is fundamental to deep learning, and optimization algorithms are the engine that drives this process. My research focuses on understanding and improving these critical algorithms.

The Current Challenge

The deep learning community has developed hundreds of optimizers, with new proposals emerging constantly. We've reached a point where determining which algorithm is truly superior—or even what "superior" means—has become increasingly difficult.

This confusion stems from our lack of a clear, theoretically grounded, and practically useful definition of what constitutes a "good optimizer."

Key Research Questions

Unified Evaluation Framework: Can we create a comprehensive criterion that balances convergence rate, empirical performance, hyperparameter robustness, and computational efficiency?

Second-Order Information: How do effective optimizers leverage matrices like the Hessian, Fisher information matrix, and covariance matrices? What patterns emerge in successful algorithms?

Practical Implementation: How can we bridge the gap between theoretical insights and practical algorithms that work efficiently at scale?

Research Approach

I combine rigorous mathematical analysis with extensive empirical evaluation. This dual approach ensures that our theoretical insights translate into practical improvements for real-world machine learning systems.

The optimization community is actively exploring these fundamental questions. My goal is to contribute meaningful advances to this critical area of AI research.

Research Objectives

1
Theoretical Foundation: Develop a mathematically rigorous framework for evaluating and comparing optimization algorithms.
2
Algorithm Analysis: Systematically evaluate existing optimizers under our new framework to identify truly superior methods.
3
Innovation: Design novel optimization algorithms that excel according to both theoretical and practical metrics.
4
Open Science: Share tools, benchmarks, and insights with the community to accelerate progress in optimization research.
